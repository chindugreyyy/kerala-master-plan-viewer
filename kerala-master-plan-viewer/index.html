      About Kerala Master Plan Viewer

      Kerala Master Plan Viewer is a web application that displays georeferenced development plans (master plans) of all cities and towns in Kerala on an interactive map. It is built for Kerala First to help citizens, planners, and researchers access planning information easily.

      Two Types of Data

      Official Master Plans: These are the actual government-approved development plans. They show future land use, zoning, roads, and infrastructure. They are the authoritative source.

      Current Land Use (Proxy): When no official plan is available, we show the current urban fabric from OpenStreetMap data. This includes buildings, roads, land use, and water bodies. It's useful for understanding the city today, but it's not a planning document.

      What is a Master Plan?

      A Master Plan (or Development Plan) is a comprehensive document that guides the future growth and development of a city or town. It includes land use zones, road networks, public spaces, and infrastructure planning.

      Have a master plan for your Kerala city/town? Click "Submit Plan" to send it to us. Plans must be georeferenced (GeoTIFF or web tiles) to be displayed.
